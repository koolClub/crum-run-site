Systems and modes

Movement first. Then hazards, loadouts, and custom tools.

The game’s identity comes from dash and phase, then builds out with escalating floors, mode variety, and a full layer of player expression.

Core movement

The two abilities that shape every route.

Dash

Dash increases speed, helps recover bad positioning, and becomes direct offense in PvP. Cooldown and duration upgrades let it scale during a run.

Phase

Phase sends the player through walls. Used alone or layered with dash, it opens routes and timing windows that would not otherwise exist.

Run pressure

Powerups, weapons, and terrain that fights back.

Powerups and weapons

Runs change with upgrades, shields, fire trails, and weapons that add more variety than a fixed-move maze loop.

Hazard floors

Goo slows movement, hot coals deal damage, and lava damages while disrupting abilities.

Moving walls

Maze routes are not always stable. Sliding walls can seal off the obvious path and force a faster adjustment.

Modes

Solo depth, co-op pressure, and local rivalry.

Solo

Progress from survival into higher-speed score and floor pushes as the run gets harder.

Local co-op

Same-screen play keeps communication and mistakes immediate. It feels built for side-by-side sessions.

Local PvP

Dash does damage, maps are generated, and pre-round choices keep matchups shifting.

Customization

Player expression is part of the game.

Hats and character setup

Adjust hat placement, rotation, scale, and character naming for each player instead of living with one locked avatar.

Emoji-based visuals

Unlock and assign emoji-based looks so the game can keep its readability while still feeling personal.

Map maker and DSP editor

Create and test maps, then save/load/share in the full version. Custom sound settings can also be saved and shared.
